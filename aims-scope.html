Aims & scope

AJRI publishes rigorous, original scholarship across the full breadth of research and innovation — from bench science to classroom practice, from clinical care to emerging technology.

Aims

What the journal aims to achieve

The American Journal of Research & Innovation (AJRI) aims to:

  • Provide an international, open-access platform for high-quality, peer-reviewed research across disciplines.
  • Foster dialogue between researchers, practitioners, educators, policymakers, and industry.
  • Accelerate the translation of research findings into practice, policy, and innovation.
  • Support early-career researchers and authors from under-represented regions through fair, constructive review.
  • Uphold the highest standards of research integrity, transparency, and publication ethics.
Scope

Subject areas

AJRI welcomes submissions in — but not limited to — the following areas:

Medicine & Clinical Research

Clinical studies, case reports, diagnostics, therapeutics, patient safety, and evidence-based practice.

Public Health & Epidemiology

Population health, health policy, global health, preventive medicine, and health systems research.

Nursing & Allied Health

Nursing science, pharmacy, physiotherapy, nutrition, and interdisciplinary healthcare practice.

Engineering & Applied Sciences

Mechanical, electrical, civil, biomedical engineering, materials science, and applied physics.

Computer Science, AI & Data

Artificial intelligence, machine learning, data science, cybersecurity, and digital health technologies.

Education & Pedagogy

Teaching innovation, curriculum design, educational technology, and academic leadership.

Business, Management & Economics

Entrepreneurship, innovation management, finance, marketing, and organizational behavior.

Social Sciences & Humanities

Psychology, sociology, policy studies, law, ethics, and interdisciplinary social research.

Environment & Sustainability

Climate science, renewable energy, agriculture, environmental health, and sustainable development.

Article Types

Accepted manuscript types

01

Original Research Articles

Full-length reports of original empirical or theoretical work.

02

Review Articles

Systematic, scoping, or narrative syntheses of the literature.

03

Case Reports

Instructive clinical or professional cases with clear learning value.

04

Case Studies

In-depth analyses of programs, organizations, or interventions.

05

Short Communications

Concise reports of preliminary or time-sensitive findings.

06

Editorials

Invited commentary on issues of scholarly significance.

07

Letters to the Editor

Responses to published work or brief scholarly observations.

08

Perspectives

Forward-looking viewpoints on emerging topics and debates.

09

Technical Notes

Descriptions of novel methods, tools, or techniques.

10

Conference Papers

Peer-reviewed papers arising from academic conferences.
